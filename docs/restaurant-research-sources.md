# Restaurant Research Sources

Use this source stack when building or auditing restaurant lists for any city.

## Required Cross-Check Sources

- Eater: current city maps, heatmaps, neighborhood guides, and price ranges when available.
- The Infatuation: current city guides, neighborhood pages, review pages, and practical booking context.
- The MICHELIN Guide: starred, Bib Gourmand, selected restaurants, cuisine type, and official price band.
- Google Maps: current existence, coordinates, neighborhood placement, hours, broad price tier, recent review volume, and obvious closure flags.
- Time Out: local critic/editor lists, neighborhood pages, and hours/pricing notes where included.
- TripAdvisor: recent traveler sentiment, price band, review volume, and warning signs around tourist traps.
- Resy / OpenTable / TheFork: reservation availability, meal period, current booking posture, and sometimes price/menu context.
- La Liste / World's 50 Best: global ranking or Discovery pages for high-end destination restaurants.
- Instagram / TikTok geo-tags: recency signal, visual reality check, hype level, and whether the place appears active.
- AllergyEats / Fig: allergy-friendly or dietary-safety evidence where available, especially for gluten-free, vegan, nut-free, or celiac-relevant picks.

## Selection Standard

Prefer a restaurant when at least two independent sources support it and one of those sources is either an editorial critic/source, an official guide, or a high-confidence platform with current hours and pricing. For high-stakes destination picks, require stronger corroboration from MICHELIN, World's 50 Best, La Liste, Eater, The Infatuation, or a comparable local authority.

Replace or downgrade a restaurant when recent sources show it is closed, mostly sustained by atmosphere over food, repeatedly flagged as poor value, outside the intended neighborhood, or weakly supported compared with nearby alternatives.

## Guide Naming

Keep SEO fields stable and keyword-forward: `slug`, `seoSlug`, `seoTitle`, and `seoDescription` should preserve searchable phrases such as "best restaurants", "best bars", or "best dive bars" for Google and route consistency.

Use `title` as the human-facing guide name. It should be contextual, specific to the neighborhood and list personality, and less generic than the SEO phrase. Avoid repeating labels like "Restaurants Worth Booking", "Best Dives", "High Volume Hits", "Culture Anchors", or "Hotels and Hostels" across neighborhoods unless there is no better local angle. Good titles should hint at why the list exists: the room style, local rhythm, scene, landmark cluster, price posture, or dominant itinerary.

## Price Tiers

- `$`: quick, casual, budget, market, counter, or mostly inexpensive meals.
- `$$`: typical sit-down restaurant, tapas bar, bistro, or moderate neighborhood meal.
- `$$$`: tasting menu, Michelin-level, high-end seafood/steak, destination dining, or consistently expensive reservations.

When sources disagree, choose the tier that best reflects a normal full meal for one person, not the cheapest possible snack or drink.
