# Stay Research Sources

Use this source stack when building or auditing hotel, hostel, hostal, guesthouse, or apartment-style stay lists for any city.

## Required Cross-Check Sources

- Hostelworld: hostel existence, dorm/private-room mix, review momentum, social programming, security notes, and backpacker fit.
- Google Maps / Google Travel: current existence, coordinates, neighborhood placement, recent review pattern, pricing posture, and closure flags.
- Booking.com / Tripadvisor: broad traveler sentiment, room-type reality, value warnings, and recent operational issues.
- Official property site: current name, direct positioning, amenities, room types, address, and whether the property still operates as described.
- Time Out / Conde Nast Traveler / The Infatuation / local hotel guides: editorial validation, design or neighborhood context, and higher-confidence hotel selections.
- Instagram / TikTok geo-tags: visual reality check, common-space energy, crowd type, and signs that the stay is still active.

## Selection Standard

Prefer a stay when at least two independent sources support it and one of those sources is either an editorial source, a property platform with current reviews, or the official property site. For hostels, prioritize location fit, recent traveler confidence, dorm/private flexibility, safety/storage basics, and whether the property is social, quiet, budget-first, or design-led.

Replace or downgrade a stay when current sources show it is closed, outside the intended neighborhood, poorly maintained, mismatched to the guide's audience, or weakly supported compared with nearby alternatives.

## Citywide Rollups

Citywide stay guides should be aggregated from the strongest pick in each neighborhood guide, not built as a disconnected list. Keep the neighborhood guides as the source of detail, then use the citywide list to help users compare the best fit by neighborhood, budget, and trip style.

## Guide Naming

Keep SEO fields stable and keyword-forward: `slug`, `seoSlug`, `seoTitle`, and `seoDescription` should preserve searchable phrases such as "best hotels", "best hostels", or "best places to stay" for Google and route consistency.

Use `title` as the human-facing guide name. It should be contextual, specific to the neighborhood and list personality, and less generic than the SEO phrase.
